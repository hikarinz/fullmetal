When Ed discovers Al's body

December 3rd, 2020

To get out of Gluttony's stomach, Edward has to transmutate the souls in the philosopher's stone that
is embedded into Greed's body. The souls serve as a "toll" to open the door of truth. Whenever
an alchemist transmutates a human, they are broken down both physically and mentally, and
become part of the flow. Becoming part of the flow means the truth of the world is forced into the
alchemist's mind. The thing about this though, is that the amount of knowledge you gain depends on
the size of your "toll". In the past Edward has only learned parts of the truth, so this time he learns a
little bit more.

The thing he "learns" in this case is the truth about his brother's body, waiting inside the doorway
of truth. Throughout the series the brothers have searched for answers on how to get the body back,
and whether it's possible in the first place. Encountering his brother's body is his first proof of that it
still exists, there is still hope. Seeing Alphonse's body, Edward begs "it" to follow him to the physical
plane, where his brother's soul is waiting. The body replies that it can't, since Edward is not his soul.
Edward is sucked in through the door to return to their world. The door shuts, and a few seconds pass
by, before Edward busts open the door of truth and tells his brother to wait, he will definitely come
back to get Alphonse.

Honestly it's extremely difficult for me to REALLY start breaking down this scene alone because it
will lead to me writing essays about the whole series. This part is very important precisely because
it's a sign of hope for Edward, which is what the character itself represents to me, not giving up, and
keep looking for the truth, because there is never only one. The way he busts open the door of TRUTH
pretty much sums up his character, if he had to be summarized (plus you have to admit, it's beyond
badass).
